Working of Quantum Logic Gates

Quantum circuits contain a lot of logic gates. It is these gates which give them their interesting computing properties. In this visualization, we explore the three purely real one-qubit logic gates.

Description of Logic Gates
Hadamard gate

This gate is mainly used for spreading and recombination of quantum states. It operates as follows:

[a,b] ⟶ [(a+b)/√2, (a-b)/√2]

Two Hadamard gates are equivalent to an identity gate, hence the Hadamard gate is its own inverse

Pauli-X or NOT gate

This gate interchanges the amplitudes of the qubit's state:

[a,b] ⟶ [b,a]

Two X gates are equivalent to an identity gate, hence the X gate is its own inverse

Pauli-Z or phase-flip gate

This gate flips the sign of the 2nd amplitude

[a,b] ⟶ [a,-b]

Two Z gates are equivalent to an identity gate, hence the Z gate is its own inverse

Measurement

If the final output is [a,b], then the probability that 0 is measured is given by a2 and the probability that 1 is measured is given by b2



Note:
  1. The quantum state of a qubit lies in a complex 2D space. The imaginary parts are not considered here
  2. Besides H, X and Z, there are other single qubit gates like Pauli-Y (Y), Phase (S,P), π / 8 (T)
  3. Multi qubit gates also exist: Controlled NOT (CNOT), Controlled Z (CZ), Toffoli (TOFF), SWAP
  4. Every quantum logic gate has an associated matrix that has to be unitary in order to preserve normalization
