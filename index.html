Effects of Special Relativity
  1. Interactive simulation of effects of special relativity. All parameters can be altered
  2. Time dilation, length contraction and relativistic doppler effect can be observed
  3. Brief explanation of the above effects and inclusion of basic formulae


Emergence in Flocking behaviour of Boids
  1. An example of emergent behaviour with three simple, togglable rules
  2. Number of boids can be altered. A specific boid's purview regions can be seen
  3. Brief description of the simple rules that give rise to complex, pseudo-mutual behaviour
  4. Radius of purview regions can be altered, except the one which may lead to crashes


Simulation of Conway's Game of Life
  1. Simulation of the classic cellular-automaton. Dimensions of the world and states of each cell can be toggled
  2. A plethora of predefined states, grouped into categories
  3. Brief introduction and interesting facts about this game


Simulation of Mandelbrot Fractal
  1. Interactive Simulation of the Mandelbrot Fractal
  2. Coloring scheme can be altered, viewing frame can be moved and rescaled
  3. Brief description about the math and the computation involved
  4. Predefined interesting regions within the Mandelbrot Set
  5. Highly optimized, but sacrifices detail


Working of Quantum Logic Gates
  1. Interactive simulation of a single quantum wire with any number of single-qubit, purely real logic gates
  2. Gates available: Hadamard, Pauli-X and Pauli-Z
  3. Amplitudes are adjustable. Standard inputs are also defined
  4. Brief description of logic gates and measurement


Visualization of Conic Sections
  1. Interactive simulation of conic sections in polar coordinate system. All parameters can be altered
  2. Circles, ellipses, parabolae and hyperbolae can be drawn
  3. Brief discussion on how they are obtained and their eccentricities


Simulation of Verhulst model of Population Dynamics
  1. Interactive simulation of population dynamics using Verhulst model
  2. The population and rate of growth are plotted with respect to time. Multiple plots can be stacked and compared
  3. Brief description and intuition of the model


Simulation of Planetary Motion
  1. Interactive simulation of gravitational interactions between any number of objects. All parameters can be altered
  2. Introduction to Universal Law of Gravitation and some of its concepts
  3. Brief explanation of gravitational fields and potentials, along with their formulae
  4. Discussion of Kepler's laws and of Stellar systems
  5. Includes pre-defined initial simulation parameters of some planetary and stellar phenomena


Visualization of Linear Transformations
  1. Interactive simulation of a linear transformation in two dimensions
  2. The matrix representing the linear transformation can be altered. Rotation matrices are automatically entered
  3. Brief description of linear transformations, determinant and inverse of a matrix
  4. The effects of a linear transformation to a particular vector can be observed


Simulation of Spring Motion
  1. Interactive simulation of spring motion. All parameters can be altered, including damping
  2. Brief introduction to Simple Harmonic Motion
  3. Practical demonstration of dependence of frequency of spring oscillations on spring constant and mass
  4. Relationship of SHM with sines and cosines
  5. Differences between ideal and actual conditions for spring motion


Operation of Heat Equation in One Dimension
  1. Interactive simulation of operation of heat equation along a single dimension
  2. Number of points and the conduction constant can be altered
  3. Brief intuition about what the equation conveys


Visualization of Trigonometric Functions
  1. Interactive presentation of all six trigonometric functions in a unit circle
  2. Existence of trigonometric functions outside of triangles and for angles more than 90 degrees.
  3. Intuition for values of trigonometric functions for multiples of 90 degrees
  4. Showcasing the odd nature of Sine and even nature of Cosine
  5. Visual proof of the three fundamental trigonometric identities


Simulation of Projectile Motion
  1. Interactive simulation of projectile motion. All parameters can be altered
  2. Brief introduction to Projectile Motion
  3. Derivation of general equation of projectile motion, time of flight, maximum height and range
  4. Demonstrating the conditions when maximum range and maximum possible heights are attained for a given initial velocity
  5. Also includes a target practice game!


Conservation of Linear Momentum
  1. Presented from the viewpoint of a Pong game
  2. Brief explanation of conservation of linear momentum and reflection on a rigid and immovable object
  3. Insight into working of the game. Calculated trajectory of the game is shown


Occurence of Stroboscopic Effect
  1. Interactive simulation of Stroboscopic Effect. All parameter can be altered
  2. Brief intuition for occurence of Stroboscopic Effect
  3. Predefined cases of stationary, wagon-wheel, near-complete aliasing and oscillating wheel
