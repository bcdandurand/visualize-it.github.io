Bifurcation Diagram of Logistic Map


A bifurcation diagram of a dynamical system depicts the long term behaviour as a function of a parameter of the system. In the below canvas, the bifurcation diagram of logistic map is plotted




Brief Description

The logistic map is represented by: \[ x_{n+1} = r \cdot x_n (1 - x_n) \]

In the above simulation, the X-axis represents \( r \) and ranges from -2 to +4. The Y-axis is supposed to depict the fixed points, but is chaotic for certain ranges of values of \( r \). For a particular value of \( r \), the above equation is repetitively iterated (starting from \( x_0 = 0.5 \) atleast 2000 times, and around 40% of the last points are plotted. A period of 1 is represented by white, and further periods are represented by blue, red, yellow and orange respectively.



Note:
  1. This simulation uses Processing and is written in p5.js
  2. The state diverges to infinity if \( r \) takes values greater than 4 or lesser than 2
